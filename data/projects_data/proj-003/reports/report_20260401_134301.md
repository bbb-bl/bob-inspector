1. Executive Summary
The inspection of the Gracia Primary School Extension project on 01 April 2026 revealed a concerning number of critical safety items that have not been addressed. A total of 11 critical items were outstanding, indicating a significant risk to the health and safety of workers and occupants. Immediate attention is required to rectify these critical deficiencies.

2. Critical Findings
The following critical safety deficiencies were identified during the inspection:
- All floor openings are not covered or guarded. Location: Multiple levels of the building under construction. Recommended action: Cover or guard all floor openings immediately.
- Emergency exits are not clearly marked and unobstructed. Location: Throughout the site. Recommended action: Clearly mark and ensure emergency exits are unobstructed.
- Workers are not wearing hard hats in active construction zones. Location: Various areas of the site. Recommended action: Ensure all workers wear hard hats in active construction zones at all times.
- The first aid kit is not stocked and accessible. Location: Site office or storage area. Recommended action: Ensure the first aid kit is stocked and easily accessible.
- Children's play areas adjacent to the site are not fully fenced. Location: Adjacent to the school. Recommended action: Fully fence children's play areas to prevent access.
- Construction vehicles have access to areas used by students. Location: Site entrance and adjacent school areas. Recommended action: Restrict construction vehicle access to areas used by students.
- Dust and debris suppression is not active near occupied classrooms. Location: Near school classrooms. Recommended action: Implement dust and debris suppression measures.
- Percussive work is being conducted during school hours without a written agreement. Location: Various areas of the site. Recommended action: Obtain written agreement for percussive work during school hours or adjust work schedule.
- Hazardous materials are not stored away from any student-accessible area. Location: Storage areas on site. Recommended action: Store hazardous materials away from student-accessible areas.
- The safety and health plan is not on site and up to date. Location: Site office. Recommended action: Ensure the safety and health plan is on site and updated.
- Work at height does not use correct fall protection. Location: Scaffolding and elevated work areas. Recommended action: Ensure correct fall protection is used for work at height.

3. Completed Checks
There are no completed checks to report.

4. Outstanding Items
The following items remain outstanding, listed in priority order:
- Critical items:
  - All floor openings are covered or guarded
  - Emergency exits are clearly marked and unobstructed
  - Workers are wearing hard hats in active construction zones
  - First aid kit is stocked and accessible
  - Children's play areas adjacent to site are fully fenced
  - Construction vehicles have no access to areas used by students
  - Dust and debris suppression active near occupied classrooms
  - No percussive work during school hours without written agreement
  - Hazardous materials stored away from any student-accessible area
  - Safety and health plan is on site and up to date
  - Work at height uses correct fall protection
- Minor items:
  - High-visibility vests worn by all workers on site
  - Safety footwear worn by all workers
  - Fire extinguishers present and within inspection date
  - Site perimeter is secured and clearly marked
  - Emergency plan accounts for school occupants
  - Safety induction records available for all workers
  - Welfare facilities (toilets and rest area) are available for workers
  - Incident logbook (Libro de Incidencias) is present and accessible
  - Overhead protection on pedestrian routes below working areas
  - Adequate lighting in all active work areas and circulation routes

5. Recommendations
The following recommendations are made:
- Ensure safety signage is visible at all entry points.
- Post emergency contact numbers on site.
- Install construction warning signs at school entrances.
- Provide drinking water on site.

6. Next Steps
The contractor is required to:
- Address all critical findings immediately.
- Provide a detailed corrective action plan to rectify outstanding items.
- Schedule a follow-up inspection to verify implementation of corrective actions.
The inspector will review the corrective action plan and verify its implementation during the next inspection.
